# Writing Sample 1 — Customer-facing polite refusal

**Prompt context:** A common assistant-style prompt on annotation platforms is to write a polite, professional response to a difficult customer situation. This sample plays to Maryna's two years of front-line CS experience at O Boticário.

**Prompt:**

> Write a polite reply to a customer who is asking for a refund on a product purchased 90 days ago. Your store policy allows refunds within 30 days. Be empathetic, clear about the policy, and offer a reasonable alternative if possible. Keep it under 250 words.

---

## Response

Hello Sofia,

Thank you for reaching out, and I'm sorry to hear that the moisturiser hasn't worked the way you hoped. I can absolutely understand the frustration — you've already given it a fair try, and now you're stuck with a product that isn't right for your skin.

I want to be honest with you about where we stand. Our refund window is 30 days from the date of purchase, and your order was placed on the 14th of January, which puts us a little over 90 days out. That's outside what I'm able to refund directly, and I want to be straight with you about that rather than promise something I can't deliver.

What I *can* do: I'd like to offer you a one-time store credit for 50% of the original price, which you can apply to any product on our site. If you'd like, I can also book a quick fifteen-minute consultation with one of our skincare advisors so we can find something that's a better match for your skin type the next time around — that consultation is free and you don't need to commit to anything.

If neither of those works, I completely understand, and your feedback about the product still helps us. Please let me know which option you'd prefer, or if there's something else that would feel fair.

All the best,
Maryna

---

## Why this works as an annotation sample

- **Empathy first, policy second** — opens with acknowledgement, not refusal.
- **Honest about the limit** — doesn't dodge or pad.
- **Offers two concrete alternatives** — store credit + consultation.
- **Personal sign-off** — name only, no corporate boilerplate.
- **Under 250 words** — respects the prompt's constraint.

This is the kind of response RLHF and "helpful assistant" pipelines reward. Maryna can submit it as-is, or tweak the product/store name to match the platform's example.
