1Profile basics & settings

Before changing any text, fix the housekeeping. These take 5 minutes and matter for searchability.

Custom URL. Current URL has the random suffix -030a21323. Go to Settings → Edit public profile & URL and change to a clean version, e.g. linkedin.com/in/maryna-bodnaryuk (if available) or linkedin.com/in/marynabodnaryuk.
Location. Set to Lisbon, Portugal. Makes her appear in Lisbon-based recruiter searches.
Industry. Set to Outsourcing & Offshoring Consulting OR Translation & Localization. Pick whichever matches her next-role target most.
Contact info. Add both phone numbers, email, and the country code so international recruiters can reach her on WhatsApp.
Public profile visibility. Settings → Visibility → make headline, summary, experience, skills, education all public.
Creator mode: OFF. Creator mode hides connection counts and adds hashtags — better for recruiters to see "Connect" not "Follow".

2Profile photo & banner

Profile photo

  • Use a clear head-and-shoulders photo, plain or soft-blurred background.
  • Smile, looking at the camera, neutral lighting (window light is best).
  • The current CV photo works well — same style for LinkedIn keeps her brand consistent.
  • Recommended size: 400×400 px minimum, square crop.

Banner image

  • LinkedIn defaults to grey — replace it. Free option: open Canva → "LinkedIn Banner" template.
  • Suggestion: a soft navy or beige banner with the words "Workforce Operations · Multilingual · AI Data & Language QA" in clean type. No stock photos of people in suits — looks generic.
  • Or: a high-quality photo of Lisbon skyline at golden hour. Personal, location-anchored, unique.

Connection-request note template

Use this short note (300-char limit)

Hi [Name] — I came across your profile while looking at [Konecta / Outlier / etc.] and wanted to connect. I'm a multilingual workforce specialist (PT/UA/RU/EN) currently exploring AI data annotation and language QA roles. Would love to be in your network. — Maryna

Engagement (light, sustainable)

  • Once a week, comment something substantive on a post by a target recruiter or platform.
  • Once every 2 weeks, post something herself — a short reflection on her career transition, a tool she's testing, a multilingual observation. Use 3–5 hashtags including #dataannotation, #aitrainer, #languageQA, #workforcemanagement, #multilingual.
  • Don't over-post. Two well-thought posts a month beats daily fluff.
